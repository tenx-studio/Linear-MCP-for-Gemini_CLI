## ✍️ Medium / Dev.to Blog Post
**Title:** Bridging the Gap: Connecting Gemini CLI to Linear with a Custom MCP Server

**Subtitle:** How I eliminated context switching by bringing my issue tracker directly into my AI terminal workflow.

If you are leaning into agentic workflows and spending most of your day in the terminal, you know the friction of the "context switch." You are deep in the zone, coding alongside an AI assistant like Gemini CLI, and suddenly you need to check a bug report, update a ticket status, or leave a comment for your product manager. 

You break your flow, open your browser, load Linear, find the ticket, make the change, and return to your terminal. Momentum lost.

I wanted to fix this. That’s why I built and open-sourced **Linear-MCP-for-Gemini_CLI**.

### What is it?
This project is a custom server built on the **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**. MCP is essentially the new standard for giving AI models secure, standardized access to external tools and data sources. 

By running this server, Gemini CLI gains native understanding of your Linear workspace. You can simply tell your terminal:
> *"Create a high-priority bug ticket for the auth module and assign it to me."*
> *"What are the open issues in the Engineering team right now?"*

### The "Auto-Connection" Magic
The biggest pain point with local AI tools is often the setup. Nobody wants to manually spin up a background Node server every time they open their terminal. 

The best part about this integration is the zero-touch lifecycle management. You simply add your compiled server path and Linear API key to your Gemini `settings.json` file. 

That’s it. 

Gemini CLI acts as the host. When you launch the CLI, it securely spins up the server in a hidden background process. When you close the terminal, it shuts it down. It is entirely seamless.

### How to get started
I have made the entire project open-source and provided a simple 4-step integration guide. You can have it running in less than 5 minutes.
